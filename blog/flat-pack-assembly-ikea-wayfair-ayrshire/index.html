Pitfalls we see every week

Most flat-pack jobs go smoothly. The bad ones share the same four or five mistakes. If you're building it yourself, dodge these; if you're getting someone in, make sure they do the same.

  • Not checking every pack before starting. We unpack everything and tick off the parts list before a screw goes in. Spotting a missing panel on page 1 saves you a wasted day when you realise on page 30.
  • Building in the wrong room. A PAX 236cm tall will not go round the top of the stairs in a Kilmarnock tenement. Build it in the room it's going to live in.
  • Not wall-fixing tall units. Every flat-pack wardrobe and tall bookcase should be anchored. We always wall-fix. In plasterboard we use proper Grip-it or Geefix anchors rated for the load — not the nasty plastic rawlplugs that come in the box.
  • Over-tightening the cam locks. They strip easily, especially on cheaper MDF. Snug is plenty.
  • Putting doors on before the carcass is square. Biggest time-waster on wardrobes. Get it square, get it level, then hang the doors — you'll spend half the time adjusting hinges.
  • Forgetting the back panel orientation. Hardboard backs often have a finished side. Once screwed on upside-down you're pulling 30 pins back out.

When flat-pack becomes a proper joinery job

Sometimes a "flat-pack" isn't. Look out for these before you buy:

  • IKEA METOD kitchens. Technically flat-pack but realistically a 2–3 day job for a full kitchen, plus worktops, plumbing and electrics. That's a joinery project, not an afternoon.
  • Sliding-door wardrobes. The doors need exact alignment and a perfectly level floor. Old Ayrshire tenement floors are rarely level — we often have to pack the base.
  • Office desks with monitor risers and cable trays. Easy build, but folk often want them wall-fixed and cable-managed, which is another hour.
  • Trampolines, sheds, bin stores. Fine in summer — grim in November when you're on wet grass. Book it in early.

Frequently asked questions

How long does an IKEA PAX wardrobe take to build?

A single 100cm PAX with two doors and internal fittings is usually 2–3 hours for us. A three-unit run with sliding doors is more like 5–6 hours because the doors have to be aligned to the millimetre.

Will you wall-fix the unit for me?

Yes. Every tall unit should be wall-fixed, especially if there are kids in the house. We'll find a stud or use a proper plasterboard fixing rated for the weight — that's included in the price.

What if a part is missing or damaged?

We'll check every pack before we start. If something's missing we'll pause, document it with photos, and either source a replacement locally or help you raise the claim with IKEA or Wayfair — whichever gets you built quickest.
