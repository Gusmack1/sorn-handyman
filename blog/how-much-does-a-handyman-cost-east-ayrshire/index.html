How Much Does a Handyman Cost in East Ayrshire?

Honest 2026 pricing for common jobs across KA postcodes — from Sorn to Kilmarnock.

One of the first things folk ask when they ring us is "how much?" — and fair enough. Nobody wants a vague "depends on the job" answer when they're trying to work out if they can afford to get that kitchen tap swapped this month. So we've put together a plain-English rundown of what a handyman typically costs in East Ayrshire in 2026, based on the jobs we actually do day to day in Sorn, Mauchline, Cumnock, Auchinleck and out towards Kilmarnock.

Hourly, half-day and day rates

Most local handyman work in KA postcodes falls into three brackets:

RateTypical rangeBest for
Hourly (1–2 hrs)£45–£60/hrOne quick job — a dripping tap, a shelf
Half-day (up to 4 hrs)£120–£200A list of small odd jobs
Full day (7–8 hrs)£220–£340Bigger tasks — decking, fit-outs, clear-outs

If you've got three or four small things piling up — a sticking door, a leaky overflow, a wobbly banister, a blind that won't go up — a half-day visit almost always works out cheaper than getting someone in four separate times. We come with the van loaded and crack through the list.

Why prices vary across KA1 to KA18

Same job, different house, different price. A few things move the number up or down:

  • Age of the property. Hanging a door in a 1980s semi in Kilmarnock is a 90-minute job. The same door in a sandstone tenement in KA1 can take half a day because the frames are out of square and the walls are lath-and-plaster.
  • Access. A second-floor flat with no lift means every bag of plaster goes up the stairs by hand.
  • Unknowns behind the wall. Old copper pipework, cloth-covered wiring, or a dot-and-dab wall with a void behind it can all add time. We flag anything we spot before we start the meter.
  • Travel. We don't charge travel within roughly 15 miles of Sorn (so Mauchline, Cumnock, Catrine, Muirkirk, Auchinleck, Galston, Kilmarnock and most of Ayr). Further afield we might add a small fuel contribution, but we'll tell you up front.

How to get the fairest quote

Three things that get you a sharper price:

  • Send a photo. A quick phone snap of the tap, the door, the damp patch — we can usually ballpark it in 5 minutes without a site visit.
  • Batch your jobs. Save up three or four small ones and book a half-day. Much better value than a ticket for each.
  • Be honest about budget. If you've got £150 to spend on a bathroom tidy-up, tell us — we'll tell you straight what's doable and what's not, rather than quoting a dream job you can't afford.

Frequently asked questions

Do you charge a call-out fee in East Ayrshire?

No. Quotes are free across KA1 to KA18 — Sorn, Mauchline, Cumnock, Auchinleck, Kilmarnock and all the villages in between. You only pay if you book the work.

Is it cheaper to pay hourly or fixed price?

For a list of small odd jobs, half-day or full-day rates usually work out better. For one clear job — hanging a door, fixing a leak — a fixed price is fairer to both sides.

Do prices include materials?

We can quote either way. Either we supply materials at cost plus a small margin, or you buy direct off a shopping list we give you.
