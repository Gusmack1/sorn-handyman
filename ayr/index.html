Ayr housing: Victorian tenements, seaside villas and newer estates

Ayr's property stock is as varied as any Scottish town. Around Wellington Square, Bellevue Crescent and Barns Street you've got big Victorian sandstone tenements and townhouses — many listed, many in the Ayr Conservation Area. Out towards Alloway and Doonfoot there are substantial red-sandstone villas and inter-war semis. Kincaidston, Heathfield and Dalmilling bring 1950s-1970s ex-council terraces and semis, and modern new-build estates keep pushing out towards Whitletts and Mount Oliphant. Each property type has its typical problems, and after years in Ayrshire we know them all.
