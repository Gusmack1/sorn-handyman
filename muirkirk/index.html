Muirkirk housing: miners' terraces, stone cottages and new-builds

Muirkirk's property mix reflects its history as an iron and coal village. Around Main Street and Glasgow Road you've got rows of traditional sandstone cottages and 1920s miners' terraces. Furnace Road and Smallburn have swathes of 1950s and 1960s ex-council housing that was built in the village's busier days, and there's a scattering of modern bungalows and new builds towards Kames and on the Cumnock-side edge. Each one has its own headaches — sash windows and lime pointing on the older stock, tired uPVC and flat-roof extensions on the mid-century terraces, and flat-pack and garden work on the newer estates.
