Galston housing: Irvine Valley sandstone, mid-century semis and new builds

Galston sits in the heart of the Irvine Valley, and the housing stock reflects it. Around the Cross and Bentinck Street you've got fine traditional Irvine Valley sandstone tenements and cottages — some listed, many in the Galston Conservation Area. Up from the Barr Castle and along Brewland Street there are big Victorian and Edwardian villas, then 1950s and 1960s ex-council semis on Waterside, Cessnock and Milrig. Modern detached developments push out off Holmes Road and Titchfield towards Hurlford. Each era has its quirks, and we're used to them all.

Typical jobs we handle in Galston

Loudoun Hill sends a lot of wind down the valley, so fencing and gutter work are regulars. We do cast-iron-to-uPVC gutter swaps on the sandstone terraces, slipped slate repairs, flat-pack assembly on the newer Holmes Road estates, bathroom and kitchen refits, laminate and LVT flooring, decking, garden sheds, painting and decorating, and landlord turnaround runs. Because we cover joinery, plumbing and painting in one visit, it often works out cheaper and quicker than calling three separate trades.
