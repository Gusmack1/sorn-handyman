Sorn Handyman Services covers Kilmarnock and the surrounding towns every week. We're Fraser and Aidan, a father-and-son handyman team based in Sorn, twenty minutes down the A71, and we work right across Kilmarnock — from the town centre and King Street, out through Bellfield, New Farm Loch, Shortlees, Onthank, Altonhill and Beansburn, and into the surrounding villages of Hurlford, Crookedholm, Galston, Kilmaurs and Stewarton. We do small jobs as happily as big ones — a squeaky floorboard is just as welcome as a full kitchen rip-out.

Kilmarnock's housing is huge in variety. The red sandstone tenements around London Road, Portland Road and John Finnie Street need specialist care — old sash windows, traditional lime-mortared stonework, cast-iron downpipes and heavy timber internal doors. Big interwar semis on Dundonald Road and around Howard Park have typical 1930s issues — timber decay in bay windows, original kitchens and bathrooms that are well past their best, gardens that need fencing and decking replacement, and solid fuel fireplaces either being opened back up or blocked in. Newer estates around Southcraigs, Moorfield and Knockentiber want flat-pack assembly, loft storage, garden sheds, painting and decorating and bathroom upgrades.

What we get asked for most in Kilmarnock: bathroom and kitchen installs, full interior painting and decorating, joinery (doors, skirting, staircases, built-in storage and fitted wardrobes), flat-pack assembly for IKEA and Wayfair deliveries, fence repairs after Atlantic storms, decking and garden rooms, flooring (laminate, LVT, engineered wood), tiling, and landlord turnarounds between tenants with a quick brilliant-white refresh. We're happy with a single task or a full snagging list — many of our Kilmarnock jobs are a mix of three or four small trades in one visit.

We know the town — Dean Castle Country Park, the Palace Theatre, Rugby Park, Burns Mall and the Howard Park are all on our regular run.
