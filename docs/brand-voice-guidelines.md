# Sorn Handyman Services — Brand Voice Guidelines

Version 1.0 · 2026-04-18 · Owner: Fraser & Aidan

This is the reference for every sentence on sornhandyman.co.uk, in emails, on printed cards, and in social posts. The point is simple: the site should read like a capable Ayrshire tradesman wrote it, not a marketing agency and not a chatbot.

---

## 1. Who we are

Sorn Handyman Services is Fraser and Aidan — a father-and-son team based in Sorn, East Ayrshire. We cover joinery, plumbing, painting, flat-pack, gutter cleaning, decking, fencing, roofing, power-washing, slabbing, landscaping, flat-pack assembly, TV wall mounting, and general property maintenance across East, South and North Ayrshire — Sorn, Mauchline, Cumnock, Catrine, Auchinleck, Galston, Kilmarnock, Ayr, Troon, Prestwick and Irvine.

Every call goes to Fraser (07900 255876) or Aidan (WhatsApp +44 7472 223323). No call centres, no sub-contractors, no algorithm routing the job to a stranger.

## 2. Audience

Ayrshire homeowners, roughly 30 to 70 years old. Practical and money-conscious. They want a trustworthy local tradesman who turns up when they say they will, gives an honest price before any work starts, and leaves the job tidy. They have been quoted by larger firms and are tired of being upsold. They read carefully and they notice the difference between writing that sounds like a person and writing that sounds like a brochure.

## 3. Voice attributes

1. **Plain-spoken** — short sentences, ordinary words, no industry jargon unless we explain it.
2. **Specific** — real towns, real postcodes, real prices, real timeframes. If we can't be specific, we say so.
3. **Honest** — we don't claim credentials we can't prove, we don't inflate experience, we don't pretend to be bigger than two people and a van.
4. **Quietly confident** — we know the trade; we don't need to shout about it. No exclamation marks unless something genuinely warrants one.
5. **Local** — we write like someone who lives four miles away. Place names, local references, weather and seasons where relevant.

## 4. Voice in practice — do and don't

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Write short sentences. Full stops are free. | Run three adjectives together (e.g. "fast, friendly, reliable"). |
| Use active verbs: "we fit", "we fix", "we replace". | Use weak verbs: "we offer", "we provide solutions for". |
| Name towns, materials, and timeframes. | Use vague filler: "areas across the region", "various services". |
| Give prices when we know them: "£45 call-out", "from £200". | Invent or imply prices we haven't quoted. |
| State facts: "based in Sorn", "father and son", "24/7 phone cover". | Pad with puff: "industry-leading", "trusted name". |
| Write British English: colour, organise, centre, kerb, tyre. | Slip into US English: color, organize, center, curb, tire. |
| Say what we do and what we don't do. | Promise "anything and everything" — it reads as AI. |
| Use double quotes for speech and quoted text. | Use single quotes except for apostrophes. |
| Use commas and full stops for pacing. | Use em-dashes to replace the logic between two ideas. One per paragraph, maximum. |
| Write "call Fraser on 07900 255876". | Write "reach out" or "get in touch with our team". |
| Address the reader as "you". | Address the reader as "our valued customer". |
| Use plain numerals: "2 hours", "£45", "10-mile radius". | Spell numbers out past ten. |
| Refer to WhatsApp, photos, and the van — specific, tangible things. | Use abstract marketing nouns: "journey", "experience", "solution". |
| Keep exclamation marks to zero per page. One is the hard ceiling, and only if the sentence demands it. | Sprinkle "!" for energy. |
| Let a paragraph be three sentences if that's the job. | Bulk sentences up to feel more substantial. |

## 5. Banned words and phrases

Avoid the following. They are the tells of AI-written marketing copy and they must not appear in body copy.

- seamless, seamlessly
- elevate, elevated
- unlock, unleash
- game-changer, game-changing
- leverage (as a verb)
- journey (as a metaphor)
- solution / solutions (as fluff — the word is fine when literally accurate, e.g. "cleaning solution")
- discover, explore
- rest assured
- we've got you covered
- reach out, don't hesitate
- in today's fast-paced world
- trusted partner, trusted name
- bespoke, tailored (unless we literally custom-built it)
- world-class, best-in-class, industry-leading
- dedicated team (we are two people)
- passionate about
- at the heart of
- empower, empowering
- cutting-edge, state-of-the-art
- robust (for processes)
- streamline
- craft (as a verb meaning "write" or "make")
- premium, luxury (unless the product literally is)

Banned patterns:

- Three-adjective stacks: "fast, reliable and friendly".
- Rhetorical questions: "Tired of waiting for tradesmen who never turn up?".
- Jokes at the end of a list: "…and if it isn't, call anyway." That reads as AI filler.
- Em-dash runs used instead of proper punctuation: "we do joinery — plumbing — painting — and more".
- "Just" and "simply" as softeners: "just give us a call", "simply send a photo".
- Listing things the reader already understands: "broken, loose, sagging, leaking, cracked, stuck…".

## 6. British English and numeric conventions

- Spelling: colour, organise, centre, labour, kerb, tyre, storey, aluminium, specialised.
- Dates: 18 April 2026, not April 18th 2026. "Today" and "next Tuesday" are fine.
- Currency: £ before the number, no space: £45, £200, £1,500. Never "$".
- Phone: 07900 255876 (mobile, space after the fourth digit). For international/tel links use the E.164 form +447900255876.
- WhatsApp number shown as: +44 7472 223323 (space after country code, space after fourth digit).
- Postcodes: upper-case with a single space, e.g. KA5 5HN, KA1, KA18. First half alone is fine where we just need the area.
- Hours: 24/7 or "open 24 hours, 7 days a week". For realistic weekday hours write "8am–6pm" with an en dash and no spaces.
- Distances: "10-mile radius", "4 miles away" — miles, not kilometres.
- Time to respond: "within the hour", "inside 24 hours", "1 to 2 weeks". Not "ASAP".
- Units: inches and feet for joinery sizing if that is what the trade uses on site (e.g. "6-foot fence panel"); otherwise metric.
- Percentages: 10%, not ten percent.
- En dash (–) for number ranges (10pm–6am, Mon–Sun). Em dash (—) for a real parenthetical, used sparingly.

## 7. Page-type voice adjustments

### 7.1 Home page
One job: get someone to either call, WhatsApp or request a quote within fifteen seconds of landing. Lead with who we are and where, list the core services, show that the photos on Facebook are real jobs. Three trust pillars, max. No big pitch paragraph.

### 7.2 Service page (`/services/<slug>/`)
Open with one paragraph that says: what the service is, what's typically included, what we charge from (if honest), and how fast we usually start. Then bullet lists for what's included and what isn't. Then a sidebar with "How we quote" steps. End with town coverage and a closing CTA. Keep sentences under 20 words. Prices anchor the page — where we have one, lead with it.

### 7.3 Area page (`/areas/<slug>/`)
Write this like you live in that town. Mention the postcode, a nearby street or landmark where reasonable, the kind of housing stock (e.g. "mostly 1960s semis" or "stone cottages on the main street"), typical jobs from that area, and driving time from Sorn. FAQs should answer the real local questions: "Do you really cover KA18?", "What's the call-out fee from Sorn to here?".

### 7.4 About page
Tell the story in normal sentences. Fraser does joinery and the adjacent trades. Aidan does plumbing, flat-pack and the paperwork. We're in Sorn. We pick up the phone. One van, one number. Don't reach for a theme.

### 7.5 Blog posts
Answer the question in the headline inside the first two sentences. Then give the detail. Write like you'd explain it at the kitchen table, with specifics and numbers. No "In this article we'll explore…" intros.

### 7.6 UX microcopy (buttons, labels, form hints)
- Buttons are verbs: "Get a free quote", "Send message", "Call Fraser".
- Placeholders are realistic examples: "e.g. Mauchline KA5", not "Enter town".
- Error messages name the field and what's wrong: "Phone number needs at least 9 digits." Not "Invalid input".
- Success messages confirm the next step: "Message sent. Fraser or Aidan will reply inside the hour."

## 8. Before / after rewrites

Each example pulls the current live copy verbatim, then rewrites it in the voice above.

### 8.1 Services index — the flagged line
**File:** `src/pages/services/index.astro:17`
**BEFORE:** "If it's broken, loose, sagging, leaking, cracked, stuck or just needs building — it's probably on this list. If it isn't, call anyway."
**AFTER:** "These are the jobs we take on most weeks. If yours isn't listed, call us anyway — chances are we've done one like it."

**Why:** the original is a seven-adjective AI-style stack followed by a punchline. The rewrite keeps the useful idea (call us for anything) but drops the gag and the stack.

### 8.2 Services index — hero line
**File:** `src/pages/services/index.astro:16`
**BEFORE:** "Fourteen trades. One number. Any hour."
**AFTER:** "Fourteen trades covered by one two-man team. Call Fraser on 07900 255876."

**Why:** the original is a three-fragment advert. The rewrite is a single plain sentence with the phone number earning its place.

### 8.3 Home page — service grid intro
**File:** `src/pages/index.astro:39`
**BEFORE:** "No job too small. If it needs fixing, fitting or freshening up, send Fraser or Aidan a photo on WhatsApp and we will come back with a free quote."
**AFTER:** "No job is too small. Send a photo on WhatsApp to 07472 223323 and we'll come back with a ballpark price the same day."

**Why:** drops the three-verb stack ("fixing, fitting or freshening up"), pins the WhatsApp number so the reader doesn't have to hunt for it, and replaces "free quote" with the more specific "ballpark price".

### 8.4 Contact page — intro
**File:** `src/pages/contact.astro:19`
**BEFORE:** "We answer every call. If it's 3am and you need help, you get a real voice. If it's 3am and you don't really, you still get a real voice."
**AFTER:** "We answer every call personally. Urgent at 3am, we're in the van. Not urgent at 3am, we'll book you in for first light."

**Why:** the original repeats "3am" for rhythm; the rewrite uses the 3am contrast to make a useful promise (what actually happens out of hours) rather than a rhetorical flourish.

### 8.5 About page — the 24/7 section opener
**File:** `src/pages/about.astro:33`
**BEFORE:** "Because problems don't respect office hours. A leaking pipe at 11pm is not waiting till Monday. A cracked pane on Christmas Eve is not a January job."
**AFTER:** "Because burst pipes don't wait for Monday. If something's actively damaging your house at 11pm, you need someone in the van that night — not a form-fill and a call back on Tuesday."

**Why:** the two-example pattern reads as writerly. The rewrite keeps the point (we turn up out of hours) and the specific (11pm burst pipe) but grounds it in what the reader actually cares about — damage happening now.

### 8.6 Areas index — closing CTA heading
**File:** `src/pages/areas/index.astro:39`
**BEFORE:** "Your town not listed? Give us a ring."
**AFTER:** "Town not on the list? Call us — we'll probably still cover it."

**Why:** the original is a rhetorical question. The rewrite gives an actual answer (we'll probably still cover it) which is both more honest and more useful.

## 9. Checklist before publishing any page

Run through this before the page ships:

- [ ] First sentence says what the page is about, no warm-up.
- [ ] Phone number and/or WhatsApp appears within the first screen.
- [ ] At least one real noun per paragraph: a town, a postcode, a timeframe, a price.
- [ ] No banned words or phrases (section 5).
- [ ] No three-adjective stacks, no rhetorical questions, no jokes at the end of lists.
- [ ] Maximum one exclamation mark on the page, and only if warranted.
- [ ] British English throughout — run a find for "color", "organize", "center", "realize".
- [ ] Prices shown as £ with no currency code, phone as `07900 255876`, postcodes uppercase with one space.
- [ ] No claims about qualifications, insurance, years of trading, family size, or awards unless Fraser or Aidan has confirmed the fact in writing and it is verifiable on the Facebook page or in a certificate on file.
- [ ] Read the page out loud. If a sentence is hard to say in one breath, shorten it.
