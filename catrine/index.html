Catrine housing: mill cottages, council terraces and new builds

Catrine's property mix tells the story of the old cotton mill village. You'll find red sandstone mill-worker cottages tightly packed along Mill Street, Newton Street and Chapel Street — most are in the East Ayrshire Catrine Conservation Area, so timber windows, slate roofs and breathable finishes matter. Up the hill you've got 1950s council terraces on Institute Avenue, St Germain Street and Montgomerie Terrace, and then modern detached and semi-detached builds creeping out towards Ballochmyle and Howford. Each era wants a slightly different touch, and we've worked on them all.

Local knowledge — council, landmarks and neighbours

We're in and out of Catrine every week. We know the AM Bar, the Co-op on Newton Street, the Voes (the big pond at the heart of the village), the old Institute, Catrine Parish Church, and the monument to the mill. East Ayrshire Council handles bin collections, building warrants and planning permissions for the village — if your job touches on a listed building or a conservation consent, we'll flag it early so you're not caught out.
